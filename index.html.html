Nielsen's 10 Heuristic Evaluation

Violations in original IRCTC identified and resolved in the redesign.

H1 · Visibility of System Status

Violation: No booking progress. Fix: 4-step progress bar (Search → Passengers → Review → Pay).

H2 · Match Real World

Violation: Railway jargon (Tatkal, GNWL, CNF). Fix: Plain-language labels with inline tooltips.

H3 · User Control & Freedom

Violation: No back/edit mid-booking. Fix: Editable summary header at each step.

H4 · Consistency & Standards

Violation: Inconsistent button styles, mismatched layouts. Fix: Unified design system.

H5 · Error Prevention

Violation: Distorted CAPTCHA causes repeated failures. Fix: Numeric OTP + social login.

H6 · Recognition over Recall

Violation: Saved trips buried deep. Fix: Recent searches and preferences surfaced on home.

H7 · Flexibility & Efficiency

Fix: PNR, cancellation, live status quick-action shortcuts on homepage for power users.

H8 · Aesthetic & Minimalist Design

Violation: Dense, cluttered homepage with 20+ links. Fix: Search-first minimal card layout.

H9 · Help Users Recover from Errors

Fix: Inline error messages with specific recovery suggestions, no dead-end pages.

H10 · Help & Documentation

Fix: Contextual tooltips for jargon terms, help bubbles, senior-assist helpline CTA.

Core Design Principles

Affordance

44px+ touch targets, clear button states, tappable cues for all interactive elements.

Visual Hierarchy

Price and availability surfaced first. Secondary info (stops, notes) collapsed by default.

Feedback

Every action has immediate visual response. Loading skeletons prevent uncertainty.

Accessibility (WCAG AA)

4.5:1 contrast ratio, screen reader labels, large text mode, disability quota auto-select.

Proximity

Related actions grouped. Payment method shown near price. Labels adjacent to inputs.

Progressive Disclosure

Minimal info on train card; full class breakdown and coach details on tap.

Competitive Analysis — 9 Platform Case Studies

Analyzed 3 tiers of travel/railway booking platforms to identify best practices and common failure patterns.

3 Best — Live Platforms

MakeMyTrip

Search-first UI, saved travellers, strong payment UX, clear availability

Adopted: Card-based results, UPI shortcuts, saved preferences

Goibibo

Minimal filters, inline availability chips, fast social login flow

Adopted: OTP login, availability tags, step-by-step progress

Cleartrip

Clean layouts, accessible date picker, mobile-first design, bottom nav

Adopted: Bottom nav, date picker design, step reduction

3 Average — Partially Live Platforms

Railyatri

Good live status data but cluttered search flow, weak hierarchy

Issue: PNR and live status buried; information overload

ConfirmTkt

Useful waitlist prediction but high login friction and no social login

Issue: Onboarding abandonment, CAPTCHA wall

RailYogi

Solid PNR tracking but inconsistent visual design across flows

Issue: Violates H4 (consistency); no trust indicators in payment

3 Worst — Partially Live / Dead Platforms

Old IRCTC (pre-2021)

Dense UI, CAPTCHA failures, 7-step booking, no mobile support

This redesign directly addresses every identified flaw

NTES Mobile

Outdated Android app, no accessibility, frequent crashes, no recovery

Violates: H1, H5, H9, H10 — total failure of feedback loops

Trainman (defunct)

Abandoned seat prediction, broken payment gateway, dead support

Trust collapse: no SSL, no refund clarity, no payment recovery

Before vs After — Flaws Identified and Resolved

BEFORE — Original IRCTC

Visual clutter

Dense homepage, 20+ links, competing CTAs, no visual hierarchy

Friction login

Distorted CAPTCHA, repeated failures, frequent session timeouts

Hidden availability

Seat status only visible after 3+ clicks into train detail page

Payment anxiety

No SSL indicators, confusing gateway redirects, 4-screen payment

No accessibility

Small targets, jargon, no screen reader support, no regional language

AFTER — Redesigned IRCTC

Search-first homepage

Prominent search card, 4 quick-action shortcuts, minimal above-fold content

Frictionless login

Mobile OTP, Google/Facebook social login, "remember me" toggle

Inline availability

Avail/WL count and fare shown on train card — zero extra clicks

Trust-first payment

SSL + PCI-DSS badges, refund policy inline, UPI one-tap shortcuts

WCAG AA accessible

44px+ targets, disability quota auto-select, plain labels, senior assist CTA

Business Impact & Roadmap

Retention & Revenue

Fewer drop-offs from 4-step flow directly increases conversion rate and ancillary sales (meals, insurance, cab).

Personalization (Roadmap)

Saved trips, tailored fare alerts, contextual offers for frequent routes — driving loyalty and repeat bookings.

Accessibility Expansion

Voice-assisted booking, 22 regional Indian languages, full WCAG 2.1 AA compliance — broadening India's 1.4B reach.

Engagement Features

Gamified loyalty check-ins, real-time delay push notifications, travel insights dashboard on roadmap.
